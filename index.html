Exploring Data Augmentation Techniques and Domain Adaptation for Low-resource Hate Speech Detection

July 2021- Dec 2021

Communicated to Elsevier(Knowledge base system)

Researched hate speech detection in low-resource languages, focusing on Hindi. Employed data augmentation and domain adaptation strategies to enhance model performance. Demonstrated significant improvements in accuracy and F1 score, outperforming existing approaches.

Identifying Offensive and Non-Offensive Contents in Multimodal Texts using Emotion and Sentiment

Jan 2022 - July 2022

Communicated to EMNLP 2023

Developed a framework for identifying offensive content in multimodal texts, with a focus on women and child-related contexts. Incorporated sentiment and emotion analysis, resulting in notable accuracy and precision enhancements.

MultiSEAO-Mix: A Multimodal Multi-task Framework for Sentiment, Emotion, Support, and Offensive Analysis in Code-mixed Setting

July 2022- Dec 2022

Communicated to TCSS

Focused on sentiment, emotion, support, and offensive analysis in code-mixed settings. Introduced the "MultiSEAO-Mix" framework, achieving significant performance gains. Created a large-scale code-mixed multimodal dataset, contributing to research in diverse linguistic expressions.

HATE Me! But WHY? Prompting for Unified Distress Identification and Cause Extraction (DICE) from Multimodal Online Posts

Jan 2022 - July 2022

Communicated to AAAI 2023

This pioneering project sought to unravel the complexities of online hostility by identifying distress signals and extracting their causal factors from multimodal online content. Leveraging a multi-task deep learning framework, we integrated NLP and computer vision techniques. The outcome is a powerful tool for understanding and mitigating online hostility, contributing to a safer digital space.
